Detailed Termite Control Services Explained

Our termite control services are designed to be thorough and effective, targeting termites at every stage of their lifecycle and ensuring your home remains protected. We tailor our approach based on the specific needs of your property and the type of termite infestation we encounter.

Comprehensive Termite Inspection

Our process begins with a meticulous inspection of your property. Our trained technicians will examine both the interior and exterior, looking for tell-tale signs of termite activity, including:

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build these tubes as protective highways from their underground colonies to their food source (your home!). We locate these tubes and follow them to determine the extent of the infestation.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume cellulose, leaving behind hollowed-out wood that may sound dull when tapped. We investigate suspicious-looking wood in basements, crawl spaces, attics, and around window and door frames.

Swarmers and Discarded Wings

Reproductive termites (swarmers) leave their colonies to establish new ones. Finding these termites or their discarded wings, often near light sources or windows, is a strong indicator of an active colony nearby.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites produce small, pellet-like droppings, often found in small piles near infested wood. Identifying these can help us determine the type of termite and the location of the infestation.

Our inspection is crucial for developing a targeted treatment plan. We utilize specialized tools and techniques to identify even hidden infestations, ensuring a comprehensive assessment.

Targeted Treatment Strategies

Based on the findings of our inspection, we implement the most effective treatment strategy. This may involve a combination of methods to achieve complete eradication and prevent future infestations.

Liquid Termiticide Barriers

We may apply a liquid termiticide to the soil around the perimeter of your home. This creates a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate. As termites attempt to cross this treated zone, they are exposed to the termiticide, which they can also carry back to the colony, effectively eliminating it.

Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems involve strategically placing bait stations in the ground around your property. Termites feed on the bait, which contains an insect growth regulator that disrupts their molting process, leading to their demise. The bait is slow-acting, allowing termites to share it with their nestmates, effectively eliminating the entire colony.

Direct Wood Treatment

In cases of localized drywood termite infestations, we may apply a termiticide directly to infested wood. This can involve injecting the termiticide into galleries or applying it to the surface of the wood. This method targets the termites within the wood and helps protect it from further damage.

We prioritize the use of environmentally responsible and safe treatments, focusing on methods that are effective against termites while minimizing impact on your family and pets. Our technicians are trained in the proper and safe application of all termiticides.

Prevention and Monitoring

After the initial treatment, we can provide recommendations and services for ongoing prevention and monitoring. This can include:

Moisture Control

Termites are attracted to moisture. We can advise on ways to reduce moisture around your foundation, such as improving drainage and repairing leaky pipes.

Wood-to-Soil Contact Reduction

Minimizing direct contact between wood and soil around your home helps prevent termites from gaining easy access.

Regular Inspections

Periodic inspections for termites are crucial for early detection. We can schedule follow-up inspections to ensure your home remains termite-free.

Our detailed service approach ensures that we not only eliminate existing termite problems but also take proactive steps to prevent future infestations, providing long-term protection for your home.

Common Termite Problems and How We Help

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation is crucial for prompt action and minimizing damage. Often, termites work silently, making their activity difficult to spot until significant damage has occurred. Understanding common termite problems can empower you to seek professional help when needed. Here are some common issues we address with our expert Termite Control in Superior, CO:

Finding Mud Tubes Around Your Foundation

Subterranean termites build these distinctive mud tubes, which are about the width of a pencil, on walls, foundation elements, and even trees. These tubes protect termites from dehydration and predators as they travel from their underground colony to their food source. If you discover mud tubes, it's a strong indication of subterranean termite activity. Our service includes identifying and tracing these tubes to locate the termite colony. We then apply targeted treatments to eliminate the colony and prevent future tube formation.

Discovering Discarded Termite Wings Near Windows or Light Fixtures

Termite swarmers, the reproductive members of a termite colony, leave the nest in large numbers to mate and establish new colonies. They often shed their wings shortly after swarming. Finding these small, translucent wings on windowsills, floors, or near light sources is a definite sign of a nearby termite colony, even if you don't see the termites themselves. Our service includes thorough inspections to find the source of the swarm, which often leads to the main colony, and then implementing effective elimination strategies.

Hollow-Sounding or Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, leaving a thin outer layer intact. This makes it difficult to detect the damage from the surface. Tapping on wood with a screwdriver and hearing a hollow sound, or noticing wood that crumbles easily, are indicators of termite damage. Our experienced technicians can expertly assess the extent of the wood damage and determine the type of termite responsible. We then apply appropriate treatments to eliminate the termites and can often recommend qualified contractors for wood repair if necessary.

Small Piles of Feces (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, live entirely within the wood they infest. They push their fecal pellets, which resemble small, hexagonal sawdust-like particles, out of the wood through kick-out holes. Finding these small piles of frass, often near a wall or piece of furniture, is a sign of a drywood termite infestation. Our service includes identifying drywood termite activity, locating the infested wood, and applying targeted treatments to eliminate the termites within the structure.

Faint Clicking Sounds Coming From Walls

Soldier termites, the protectors of the colony, bang their heads against wood or tunnel walls when disturbed. This creates a faint clicking or rustling sound within infested walls. While this sound can be difficult to distinguish from other household noises, a trained ear can sometimes detect it. If you suspect termite activity and hear unusual sounds within your walls, it's worth having a professional inspection. Our technicians can use specialized listening devices to help confirm the presence of termites within wall voids and then implement targeted treatments to eliminate them.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

In severe termite infestations, significant structural damage can occur, leading to sagging floors, ceilings, or warped walls. This level of damage indicates a long-standing and extensive termite problem that requires immediate professional attention. Our service includes a complete assessment of the damage, identification of the active termite colonies responsible, and implementation of aggressive treatment strategies to stop further damage. We can also provide recommendations for structural repairs once the termite issue is resolved.

Blistering Paint or Wallpaper

As termites tunnel through wood just beneath the surface, they can cause paint or wallpaper to bubble or blister. This is due to the moisture they introduce or the changes they make to the wood's surface. If you notice unexplained bubbling or blistering on your walls, especially in areas prone to moisture, it could be a sign of termites. Our inspections include checking for these visual cues, which can help pinpoint hidden termite activity and guide our treatment efforts.
